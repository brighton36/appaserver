Output Model Compare Datatypes

This process compares the model results of one datatype with one or more other datatypes from either the same alternative or different alternatives.

  • Begin and End Dates:
    • Leave both blank will report the entire period of record.
    • Enter a full Begin Date and End Date will report on that date range.
    • Enter only the year in Begin Date will report from January 1.
    • Enter only the year in End Date will report to December 31.
    • Enter only the year and month in Begin Date will report from the first day of that month.
    • Enter only the year and month in End Date will report to the last day of that month.
    • Enter a full Begin Date and leave the End Date blank will report on the single day.
    • Enter a only the year in Begin Date and leave the End Date blank will report on the entire year.
    • Enter a only the year and month in Begin Date and leave the End Date blank will report on the entire month.
    • Leave blank the Begin Date but populate the End Date will report from the beginning of record to the End Date.
  • Aggregate Level: Optionally select an aggregate level to compare sums (if quantum datatypes) or averages (if flow datatypes).
    • Weekly: Model results between the date range will be aggregated per week.
    • Monthly: Model results between the date range will be aggregated per month.
    • Annually: Model results between the date range will be aggregated per year.
  • Output Medium:
    • Table: A table is displayed in the bottom window.
    • Chart: A chart is displayed in a new window.
    • Text File: A text file is created to transmit to your computer.
    • Correlation:
      • Correlation: the Pearson Coefficient of Correlation (R) is a number between -1 and 1. It is used to predict how the value of one datatype affects the value of another datatype. -1 represents a completely inverse relationship; 0 represents no relationship, and 1 represents a completely positive relationship.
      • Determination: the Coefficient of Determination (R squared) is a number between 0 and 1. It measures the explaination of the affect one datatype has on another. A value of 0 means one datatype has no affect on another. A value of 0.50 means one datatype has a 50% affect on another, with another 50% explained by some other means. A value of 1 means one datatype as a complete affect on another.
      • Covariance: an unbounded measure of the affect one datatype has on another. A large negative number represents a strong inverse relation ship, zero represents no relationship, and a large positive number represents a strong positive relationship.
      • Average, Count, and Standard Deviation: these statistics are provided to add perspective to the measures of relationship.
      • Note: The first row is the one datatype compared with itself. The correlation and determination should be 1 (or very close to 1), and the covariance should be a positive number.
    • Scatter: For each datatype compared with, a scatter chart is produced. The scatter chart also displays the formula for producing the line of least squares (linear regression).
